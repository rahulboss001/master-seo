6.1

Backlink Quality, Types & Evaluation Criteria

Intermediate▼
Not all backlinks are equal — and this is the single most important concept in link building. One contextual editorial link from a DR 75 industry publication is worth more than 10,000 low-quality directory links. Understanding the exact criteria that determine backlink value lets you focus your link building effort where it produces maximum ranking impact, and avoid wasting time on links that do nothing (or worse, cause penalties).
Domain Authority (DR/DA) — The authority multiplier: Ahrefs Domain Rating (DR) and Moz Domain Authority (DA) are 0–100 scores estimating a site's link equity. DR 80+ = premium (think Forbes, TechCrunch, major news outlets). DR 40–79 = strong (industry blogs, established niche sites). DR 20–39 = decent for newer sites. Under DR 20 = minimal ranking impact. Note: Google uses its own proprietary PageRank — DR/DA are strong proxies but not exact equivalents.
Topical Relevance — Often more important than authority: A DR 50 link from a fitness blog to a fitness equipment store is more valuable than a DR 80 link from a car dealership. Google's algorithm evaluates the topical relationship between the linking page, the linking site, and your site. The ideal link: high DR site, topically relevant niche, contextually embedded in relevant content.
Link Placement — Where on the page matters: Editorial links (naturally embedded in article body text) = maximum value. Sidebar/footer links = less value (these often appear site-wide and are treated as lower-quality). Comment section/forum signature links = usually nofollowed, minimal impact. The gold standard: a link in the first 300 words of a relevant article body.
Anchor Text Distribution — The manipulation detector: Keyword-rich anchors ("buy running shoes online") pass more contextual relevance but a pattern of all-exact-match anchors is a manipulation signal that Google's Penguin algorithm flags. Natural anchor distribution: ~40% branded (your site name), ~30% partial-match, ~20% generic ("click here", "read more"), ~10% exact keyword. Build naturally — don't engineer anchor text ratios.
DoFollow vs NoFollow vs UGC vs Sponsored: DoFollow = passes full link equity. NoFollow (rel="nofollow") = historically zero equity, now Google treats as a hint (may pass some value). Sponsored (rel="sponsored") = paid relationship disclosed. UGC (rel="ugc") = user-generated content. A natural backlink profile has a healthy mix. Sites that only have dofollow links look artificial.

6.2

Guest Posting — The Relationship-Based Link Acquisition System

Intermediate▼
Guest posting — writing content for other websites in exchange for a backlink — remains one of the most reliable and scalable link building tactics when done correctly. Google's stance: guest posting purely for links at scale is against guidelines; guest posting to build genuine audience, brand authority, and relationships (with a contextual link as a natural byproduct) is fine. The difference is quality and intent. One exceptional guest post on a DR 60 site beats 50 mediocre posts on DR 15 sites.
Finding Guest Post Opportunities: Google search operators: "[your niche] + write for us", "[niche] + guest post", "[niche] + contribute", "[niche] + submit article". Also: Ahrefs → Competing Domains → find sites linking to competitors but not you → check if they accept guest posts. Aim for DR 30+ with genuine audience relevance.
The Perfect Pitch Formula: Subject: specific + personalised. Body: (1) one genuine compliment about their content (reference a specific article), (2) one gap you spotted in their coverage, (3) three specific article title options addressing that gap, (4) one sentence of your relevant credentials. Total: under 150 words. Never send generic pitches — editors receive hundreds and reject everything that's clearly templated.
Writing to Win Future Opportunities: Your guest post should be better than their average content — use original examples, real data, or first-person experience. Editors who love your work will invite you back, give you author bios with dofollow links, and sometimes link to your content in future articles without you asking. One great relationship beats 100 cold pitches.
Where to Place Your Link: Always aim for a contextual link within the article body (not just the author bio). Contextual links pass significantly more equity. Frame your link naturally: "For a step-by-step guide to [topic], [anchor text]." Avoid using exact-match keywords as anchor text — use partial-match or branded anchors to stay natural.

An SEO freelancer systematically pitched 60 guest posts over 3 months targeting only DR 40+ sites in the digital marketing niche. Acceptance rate: 13 out of 60 (22%). Average DR of accepted sites: 58.

From those 13 guest posts, her main site's DR increased from 21 to 34. Target keywords moved an average of 9 positions. She also received 3 inbound consulting inquiries from editors who became clients — making guest posting a lead generation tool, not just a link building tactic.

6.3

Broken Link Building — Replacing Dead Resources with Yours

Intermediate▼
Broken link building is one of the highest-conversion link outreach tactics because you're offering genuine value — you're helping a webmaster fix a problem on their page (a dead link) while suggesting a natural replacement. The psychology is completely different from a cold link request: you're doing them a favour first. Conversion rates of 8–20% are common with well-executed broken link campaigns, compared to 1–5% for cold link requests.
Step 1 — Find resource pages with broken links: Google: [your niche] + "resource page", [niche] + "useful links", [niche] + "recommended resources". Resource pages are link goldmines — they aggregate external links and regularly accumulate dead ones as old content disappears from the internet.
Step 2 — Find the broken links: Install the "Check My Links" Chrome extension. Visit any resource page and it instantly highlights all broken links in red. Or use Ahrefs → Broken Link Checker on any domain to find all outgoing broken links at scale.
Step 3 — Create or identify your replacement content: Use the Wayback Machine (web.archive.org) to see what the dead page used to contain — then either create a replacement resource or identify an existing piece of your content that covers the same topic better.
Step 4 — Send the outreach email: Keep it short and genuinely helpful. Lead with "You have a broken link on [specific page URL]" — this immediately proves you've done work they haven't. Offer your replacement. Never be pushy or transactional. Conversion rate increases significantly when you mention multiple broken links on the same page.
📖 Broken Link Campaign Results

An SEO consultant ran a systematic broken link campaign targeting "digital marketing resources" pages. Using Ahrefs, they identified 340 resource pages with broken outgoing links. They contacted 180 webmasters over 3 weeks.

Results: 28 placements (15.5% conversion). Average DR of linking sites: 44. Campaign cost: 0 tools cost (used Ahrefs trial), ~25 hours of work. The 28 links contributed to 3 target keywords moving from page 2 to top 5 within 10 weeks.

6.4

Digital PR & HARO — Earning Editorial Links from Major Publications

Advanced▼
Digital PR and HARO (Help A Reporter Out — now Connectively) represent the highest-ROI link building tactics available for service and knowledge businesses. A single mention in Forbes, TechCrunch, or a major industry publication (DR 85–95) provides more ranking power than months of other link building combined — and they're completely free. The skill required: positioning yourself as a credible expert source and responding quickly to journalist queries with genuinely useful, quotable insights.
How HARO / Connectively Works: Journalists from publications of all sizes post daily queries seeking expert sources, data, or quotes. You respond as an expert in your niche. If selected, you get an editorial mention with a high-authority backlink. Three emails per day: morning, afternoon, evening. Respond within 1–3 hours for best results — journalists work on deadlines.
Writing a Winning HARO Response: (1) Directly answer the journalist's question in the first sentence — don't bury the lead. (2) Include a compelling statistic or data point if available. (3) Keep responses under 200 words unless more detail is requested. (4) End with your full credentials (name, title, company, brief bio). Never pitch your business — journalists want expert insight, not advertising.
Digital PR — Proactive Outreach: Beyond HARO, create news-worthy content or data that journalists naturally want to reference: original research (even small surveys), industry reports with surprising data, counterintuitive findings, visualised data stories. Then pitch directly to journalists covering your industry via Twitter/X (many journalists are active there and accept pitches in DMs).
Building a PR Asset Library: Prepare in advance: headshot, short bio (50 words), long bio (200 words), key statistics you know cold, 3–5 go-to talking points in your niche, and links to previous media mentions. When a journalist needs a quick quote, being ready in 5 minutes beats spending 30 minutes crafting a response — by which time they've moved on.
📖 HARO Success Story

An independent financial advisor monitored HARO daily for 3 months, responding to every relevant query about retirement planning, investments, and personal finance — roughly 12 responses total.

Results: 4 mentions in Forbes.com (DR 94), 2 in NerdWallet (DR 87), 1 in The Guardian (DR 94). Total: 7 high-authority editorial links. Budget: $0. Time: ~30 minutes per week.

Their keyword "retirement planning advisor" moved from position 18 → position 6 as a direct result. Brand search volume increased 340% over the 3-month period.

6.5

Linkable Assets — Content That Earns Links Passively

Advanced▼
A linkable asset is a piece of content so genuinely valuable, comprehensive, or unique that other websites link to it without you having to ask. This is the most scalable link building strategy: create it once, promote it briefly, and let it compound over years. The best linkable assets become the #1 cited resource in their niche, earning hundreds or thousands of links on autopilot — while every piece of content you publish from the same domain benefits from the authority they transfer.
Original Research & Industry Data: Commission or conduct a survey of 100+ people in your niche. Publish the findings with visualised charts. Example: "We surveyed 500 SEO professionals about their 2025 link building tactics — here's what we found." Journalists, bloggers, and researchers constantly need data to cite — yours becomes the primary source. One study can earn 50–500+ citations over 2–3 years.
Free Tools & Calculators: Build a simple tool that solves a recurring calculation problem in your niche. Examples: mortgage payment calculator (finance), calorie deficit calculator (health), SEO ROI calculator (marketing). Free tools attract links from educational content, comparison articles, and "best free tools" roundups. They rank for long-tail "calculator" keywords and link naturally to your main service pages.
Comprehensive Definitive Guides: A 10,000+ word pillar resource that covers every aspect of a topic — significantly more comprehensive than any existing resource. Example: "The Complete Guide to Fermentation" for a food blog. When this becomes the definitive reference for a topic, every article written about that topic has reason to link to it.
Curated Data Visualisations & Infographics: Take complex data (industry statistics, process maps, comparison charts) and make it visually compelling. Infographics get shared and embedded — often with backlinks. Include an embed code to make it frictionless for others to share your asset (with a link back to you as the source).
📖 Linkable Asset — Real Scale

A software company published "The State of Remote Work 2024" — a survey of 1,200 remote workers with 40+ data points visualised in charts. Promotion effort: pitched 50 journalists via email and Twitter over 2 weeks.

Results over 12 months: 847 referring domains. Featured in: Harvard Business Review, Forbes, TechCrunch, BBC, and 200+ niche blogs. Domain Rating increased from 41 to 67. The asset now auto-earns 15–25 new links every month with zero outreach — journalists discover and cite it organically from other citations.

6.6

Toxic Links, Penalties & the Disavow Strategy

Advanced▼
Not every backlink helps you — some actively hurt your rankings. Google's Penguin algorithm (running continuously since 2016) can demote sites with unnatural, manipulative link profiles. Knowing how to identify toxic links, when to disavow, and how to recover from link-based penalties is the defensive side of link building. Any SEO professional inheriting an existing site must conduct a toxic link audit — especially if the site was worked on by unknown or black-hat agencies.
What Makes a Link Toxic: Links from Private Blog Networks (PBNs — sites built purely to sell links), exact-match keyword anchor text at unnatural scale, links from completely irrelevant industries (a gambling site linking to a kids' toy store), links from hacked sites, links from sites with manual actions in Google's index, sitewide footer links with commercial anchors across thousands of pages.
How to Identify Toxic Links: Run a backlink audit in Ahrefs or SEMrush. Red flags: large numbers of links from the same IP subnet (indicating a PBN), links with 100% exact-match keyword anchors, links from sites with DR 0-5 and zero organic traffic, links from adult or gambling sites to unrelated niches, sudden large spikes in backlink acquisition.
The Disavow Process: Google Search Console → Links → Disavow (via Legacy Search Console). Create a disavow .txt file listing domains or URLs to disavow. Google then ignores these links when evaluating your site. Important: disavow is a last resort — only for clearly manipulative links. Don't disavow links just because a site has low DR.
Manual vs Algorithmic Penalties: Manual action = a Google reviewer has directly penalised your site (appears in GSC → Manual Actions). Requires submitting a reconsideration request after fixing the issues. Algorithmic (Penguin) = automatic scoring downgrade. No notification — you just see rankings drop. Fix is the same (remove/disavow toxic links) but recovery happens at the next algorithm refresh.
⚠️ Disavow Caution

Many SEOs are too aggressive with disavow — they disavow links that are actually neutral or beneficial out of fear. Only disavow links that are clearly manipulative: bought links, PBN links, or links that coincide with a known penalty.

Incorrectly disavowing good links can actively hurt your rankings. When in doubt, leave it alone. Google's algorithm is sophisticated enough to discount most low-quality links without your help.

Google's John Mueller has confirmed that most sites don't need to use disavow at all — Penguin handles low-quality links automatically for most sites.

❌ The answer is B. Contextual editorial links in article bodies on topically relevant sites pass maximum equity. Sitewide links are discounted, nofollow links pass minimal value, and irrelevant sites lose topical relevance signals regardless of DR.
